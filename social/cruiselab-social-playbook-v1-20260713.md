# The Cruise Lab — Social Media Strategy & Playbook

**Version 1 — 13 July 2026**
Owner: Stuart Brodie · Constraints: fully faceless brand, 3–5 hours/week
Goal ranking: 1. Follower/view growth → 2. Traffic to the tools → 3. Affiliate revenue → 4. Forever Voyage leads

---

## 1. The core strategic insight

The Cruise Lab is not a travel vlog. It is a **data brand**. Nobody follows a faceless account for "look at this nice ship" — there are ten thousand of those. People follow, share and save content that tells them something they didn't know and couldn't easily find out. The Cruise Lab already owns exactly that:

- ~70,000 individually scored cabins across Princess and MSC (GCBC)
- The maths behind casino comp points (Sea You Soon)
- Real predicted bar spend vs package prices (GetMyBarTab)
- Which eSIMs actually work at open sea — a fact most guides get wrong (GetMyCruiseConnection)
- Historical weather for 265 cruise ports (GetMyCruiseWeather)
- 260-port editorial database (Shore Thing)
- Deck-plan geometry: what's above, below and beside every cabin

**Positioning statement:** *The Cruise Lab is the account that runs the numbers on cruising.* Every post is a finding, a verdict, a ranking, a myth-bust or a "the data says" — delivered in the lab's voice, never a person's. The flask is the face.

This is also the ideal faceless posture: screen recordings, animated charts, deck-plan graphics, text-on-b-roll and voiceover carry everything. The 2026 research is unambiguous that faceless informational accounts scale faster than personality accounts because they're systems — consistency beats charisma, and hooks beat production quality.

**Banner (YouTube/Facebook/X):** navy field, gold flask left, Cinzel wordmark, tagline right.

**Tagline for bios:** "Free web tools built by a cruiser, for cruisers." Bio template:

> ⚓ The account that runs the numbers on cruising
> 🧪 Cabin scores · bar tabs · casino points · port guides
> 🔗 All free tools ↓

**Visual language on social:** the same tokens as the web estate — navy backgrounds, champagne gold accents, Cinzel for big display numbers/verdicts, Outfit for body text, sea-glass for highlights. **Sign-off convention:** every video ends on a 1–2 second gold-on-navy end card: flask + "thecruiselab.com" + tool name. This is your only branding moment — don't waste footage on intros; hooks come first, brand comes last.

## 3. Platform strategy

Cruise audiences are two overlapping groups: the **planners** (45–70, heavy Facebook and YouTube users, high spend, join cruise Facebook groups by the hundred-thousand) and the **dreamers/first-timers** (25–45, TikTok and Reels, where cruise content routinely goes viral). Growth (goal #1) lives in short-form video; the cruise money demographic lives on Facebook and YouTube. The play is **one short-form production system distributed everywhere**, plus one community platform.

### Tier 1 — the short-form triad (same videos, cross-posted)
| Platform | Role | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **TikTok** | Growth engine #1 | Discovery-first algorithm gives reach from zero followers; ~70% of users search travel content in-app. Post natively, use its text/caption tools. |
| **Instagram Reels** | Growth engine #2 + the "home base" profile | Also post carousels here (rankings, before/after cabin comparisons) — carousels get outsized saves in 2026. Grid = brand shopfront. |
| **YouTube Shorts** | Growth + long-tail search | Shorts feed the channel; YouTube search keeps videos earning views for years ("best MSC Yacht Club cabin" is a search query, not a trend). |

### Tier 2 — community & distribution (low effort, high leverage)
| Platform | Role | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Facebook Page + Reels** | Where the cruise demographic actually is | Cross-post the same Reels. The Page exists mainly so content can be *shared into cruise groups* — Facebook cruise groups (Princess, MSC, line-specific, "Cruise Tips" groups) are enormous and are the single most concentrated pool of your exact users on the internet. |
| **Pinterest** | Evergreen tool traffic (goal #2) | Not a social network — a visual search engine. Pins of "cabin picking checklist," port guides, packing/connectivity infographics drive steady clicks to tools for years. 30 min/week, scheduled. |

### Tier 3 — reserve the handle, minimal effort
- **X/Threads/Bluesky:** claim handles, auto-share launches and findings occasionally. Not worth active investment for this niche at 3–5 hrs/week.
- **LinkedIn:** personal-account posts only when there's a "solo dev builds cruise tool empire" story worth telling (this occasionally travels far in indie-hacker circles and is a different audience — optional).
- **Reddit (r/Cruise, r/cruises, line subreddits):** not a posting channel — a *listening and genuine-participation* channel. Answer questions helpfully, link tools only when directly relevant and allowed. Reddit hates marketing and loves useful free tools; play it long.

**What we're deliberately not doing:** long-form YouTube (too expensive at 3–5 hrs/week — revisit at 10k subs), livestreams, X as a strategy, paid ads (revisit once organic formats are proven — then small boosts on winners only).

## 4. Content pillars

Five pillars, each mapped to owned data. Every piece of content belongs to exactly one pillar. Rotate through them.

**P1 — Cabin Verdicts (GCBC)** — the flagship pillar, most differentiated.
"We scored all 2,200 cabins on this ship. This is the worst one." Deck-plan zooms, what's-above-you reveals, GOOD/BAD stamps, best-cabin-for-£X, "never book a cabin under the gym" with the actual deck plan as proof.

**P2 — The Bar Tab (GetMyBarTab)**
"Is the drinks package actually worth it? We did the maths." Itemised-tab reveals, light vs heavy drinker scenarios, "the break-even is 6 drinks a day — here's what that looks like."

**P3 — Connected at Sea (GetMyCruiseConnection)**
Myth-bust gold: "Your travel eSIM will NOT work at sea. Here's why." Airplane-mode horror-bill stories, Starlink explainer, the two eSIMs that actually work mid-ocean.

**P4 — Port Intel (Shore Thing + GetMyCruiseWeather)**
"Docked vs tender — why it matters." Don't-miss lists per port, "what's the weather actually like in Cozumel in September? We checked 10 years of data." Rankings: walkable ports, overrated ports, private islands compared.

**P5 — Casino Points & Cruise Maths (Sea You Soon + general)**
"How much do you have to gamble to earn a free cruise? The actual numbers." Comp-point mechanics, points-per-day pacing, general cruise-pricing maths.

**Recurring series names** (series = follow triggers): *Cabin Court* (P1 verdicts), *The Tab* (P2), *Lab Report* (any data finding), *Port File* (P4). Consistent title cards per series.

## 5. Formats & the hook library

All formats are faceless: screen recording + zoom/pan, static graphics with kinetic text, stock/free b-roll underneath text, AI or own voiceover (a calm Scottish voiceover is a brand asset if you're comfortable recording voice only — otherwise ElevenLabs). Videos: 15–40 seconds. **The first 2 seconds decide everything** — algorithm distribution in 2026 hinges on hook retention, saves and shares, not production polish.

### Format recipes (repeatable templates)

**F1 — The Verdict** (P1, 20–30s)
Hook: "This is the worst cabin on [ship]." → deck-plan zoom to the cabin → 3 rapid reasons with icons (above: nightclub; beside: crew door; motion: bow) → GOOD/BAD stamp slams down → end card. *Infinitely repeatable: every ship has a worst and best cabin, a best-value balcony, a secret gem.*

**F2 — The Receipt** (P2, 25–40s)
Hook: "You think the drinks package saves you money. Watch." → itemised bar tab prints line by line (the tool's literal output — screen record it) → total vs package price → verdict. 

**F3 — The Myth-Bust** (P3/P5, 15–25s)
Hook: "Everyone gets this wrong about cruise WiFi." → the wrong belief on screen, struck through → the correct fact → one-line why → end card.

**F4 — The Ranking** (P4, 30–40s video or IG carousel)
Hook: "5 cruise ports you can walk off the ship into. No taxi. No tour." → countdown with one b-roll clip + one fact each → #1 held longest. Carousel version for Instagram = saves machine.

**F5 — The Lab Report** (any pillar, 20–30s)
Hook: "We analysed [big number] [things]. Here's what nobody tells you." → 3 findings as animated stat cards → end card. Big proprietary numbers (70,000 cabins, 265 ports, 10 years of weather) are the hook.

**F6 — The Tool Demo (sparingly — 1 in 6 posts max)**
Hook is the *outcome*, never the tool: "Find out your exact bar spend in 30 seconds" → fast screen recording → result reveal → "free, link in bio." Demos convert but don't grow; the ratio matters.

### Hook formulas (rotate; A/B relentlessly)
- "This is the worst ___ on ___" / "Never book ___ without checking ___"
- "We ran the numbers on ___ so you don't have to"
- "Everyone gets this wrong about ___"
- "The cruise line won't tell you this, but ___" (use sparingly, stay factual)
- "£___ vs £___: which cabin would you pick?" (comment-bait, drives engagement signals)
- "POV: you booked cabin ___ and this is above you"

### Sound & text rules
- Design for **sound-off first**: every word on screen. Trending audio *underneath* at low volume where it fits (faceless videos with trending sounds share measurably better).
- Captions always on. One idea per video. No intros, no "welcome back."

## 6. The production system (fits in 3–5 hours/week)

The accounts that win are systems, not inspiration. One batch session, one distribution routine.

**Weekly rhythm:**

| Block | Time | What |
|---|---|---|
| **Batch session** (e.g. Sunday) | 2–2.5 h | Pick 4 ideas from the idea bank (one per pillar, rotating). Script each in 4 lines: hook / body / verdict / CTA. Capture assets (screen recordings of the tools, deck-plan crops, b-roll pulls). Assemble in CapCut using saved templates. Export 4 videos + 1 carousel variant. |
| **Distribution** (15 min/day, 4 days) | 1 h | Post 1 video/day natively to TikTok, Reels, Shorts, Facebook (scheduling tools can automate the fan-out; post TikTok natively for best treatment). Platform-tune the caption + 3–5 niche hashtags (#cruisetok #cruisetips #cruisehacks + line/ship tags). |
| **Engagement** (10 min/day) | ~1 h | Reply to every comment in the first hour after posting (early engagement signals drive distribution). Answer questions with answers, not links. Drop replies in 2–3 Facebook groups / Reddit threads where a tool genuinely answers someone's question. |
| **Pinterest** (weekly, batched) | 20 min | 3–5 pins from the week's graphics, keyword-rich descriptions, linking to the relevant tool. |

**Toolchain:** CapCut (templates, captions), Canva or hand-built HTML/SVG graphics in the design system (you can literally build stat-card animations as HTML and screen-record them — on brand and free), ElevenLabs or own voice-only recordings, screen recordings via QuickTime, free b-roll from Pexels/own cruise footage archive. Build **one reusable CapCut template per format (F1–F6)** in week 1 — this is what makes 4 videos in 2.5 hours possible.

**Idea bank:** keep a running list (a simple `social/ideas.md` in the monorepo). Every ship in GCBC yields 5+ Cabin Court videos; every Shore Thing editorial batch yields Port Files; every tool feature yields a demo. You will never run out — the estate *is* the content calendar.

## 7. The funnel (goals 2–4 without hurting goal 1)

- **Link in bio → a self-hosted link hub.** Don't use Linktree — build `links.thecruiselab.com` (or `/links` on the hub): a single Cruise Lab-styled page with one button per tool, in Fleet order, plus Forever Voyage at the bottom. On-brand, free, and you keep the analytics. *(Small build task — an hour in the design system.)*
- **CTA discipline:** growth content (F1–F5) ends with the end card only — no hard sell. Tool demos (F6) say "free, link in bio." Never more than one CTA per video.
- **Affiliate rules on social:** any video whose linked destination carries affiliate links gets `#ad`-adjacent honesty — a "partner links on site" line in the caption is sufficient since the video itself isn't sponsored; keep the existing on-site disclosures doing the heavy lifting. Never state commission mechanics in content (existing estate convention).
- **Forever Voyage:** don't market it in the growth feed. One tasteful "we build keepsake cruise websites" post per month on Facebook/Instagram, and the link-hub button. Its buyers arrive via trust in the brand, not via TikTok.
- **Cruise line IP caution:** ships, deck plans and line names can be discussed factually (reviews/commentary), but keep the existing independence disclaimer in bios, don't use line logos in thumbnails/graphics, and stay factual in "won't tell you this" framings. Same posture as the web estate.

## 8. Metrics & review

Weekly 10-minute check, monthly 30-minute review. Track in a simple spreadsheet or `social/metrics.md`.

**North-star (goal 1):** views/week and follower growth rate per platform.
**Leading indicators:** hook retention (3-second hold %), saves and shares per post (the 2026 distribution currencies), watch-through %.
**Funnel (goals 2–3):** link-hub clicks (add a lightweight counter or use Cloudflare analytics), per-tool referral traffic, affiliate clicks.
**Monthly ritual:** rank the month's posts, identify the top 20%, make more of exactly that; kill or rework the bottom format. Double down on whichever platform is breaking out — at 3–5 hrs/week you earn the right to go deeper on one platform by proving it first.

**Honest expectations:** faceless accounts posting consistently typically see traction in 30–60 days; a realistic aim is a few thousand followers in the first 90 days with one or two outlier videos doing most of the work. The outliers are why hook variety and volume matter — every post is a lottery ticket the algorithm tests for you.

## 9. 90-day launch plan

**Week 1 — Foundations (one-off ~4 h)**
1. Register `@thecruiselab` (or fallback) on TikTok, Instagram, YouTube, Facebook, Pinterest, X, Threads, Bluesky. Business/creator accounts where offered.
2. Upload avatar/banner/bios everywhere (identical).
3. Build `links.thecruiselab.com` link hub; set as the bio link everywhere.
4. Build CapCut templates for F1, F3, F5 (start with three formats, add F2/F4/F6 in week 3).
5. Seed the idea bank with 30 ideas (6 per pillar).
6. Create `social/` folder in the monorepo: `ideas.md`, `metrics.md`, brand assets, this playbook. Update master context to rev 14 recording the social estate.

**Weeks 2–5 — Establish the system**
- 4 posts/week across the triad + Facebook. Lead with P1 Cabin Verdicts (most differentiated) and P3 myth-busts (most shareable).
- Reply to everything. Note which hooks hold attention.
- Pinterest: 3 pins/week.

**Weeks 6–9 — Iterate**
- First monthly review: kill weakest format, double the strongest.
- Introduce IG carousels (F4 rankings) and the first F6 tool demos.
- Start genuine Facebook-group/Reddit participation (answers first, links when asked/relevant).

**Weeks 10–13 — Amplify**
- Ride the winners: turn the best-performing video into a series (same format, new ship/port each time).
- First "Lab Report" milestone post ("you helped X people check their cabin this month" — community flywheel).
- Assess: which platform earned deeper investment? Consider a £20–50 boost test on the single best Facebook Reel (cruise demo is cheap to reach there).
- Write rev 15 notes: what the data said, what changes in quarter two.

## 10. Operating rules (the discipline list)

1. Faceless always — the flask is the face; voice-only is permitted, face never required.
2. One idea per video. Hook in 2 seconds. Brand at the end, never the start.
3. Never post without a pillar. Never demo the tools more than 1 post in 6.
4. Same handle, avatar and voice on every platform.
5. Reply to comments within the first hour of posting.
6. Batch production weekly; never create day-of.
7. Every claim must be backed by the estate's data — the brand *is* trust in the numbers.
8. Monthly review is sacred: the data decides the content, same as everywhere else in the Lab.
9. Disclosures stay on; commission mechanics stay out of copy (estate convention).
10. When in doubt: would a cruiser save this? Saves and shares grow accounts; likes are vanity.
