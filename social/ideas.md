# The Cruise Lab — Idea Bank v1

15 July 2026. A standing well of video ideas so no batch session starts from a blank page. **30 ideas, 6 per content pillar.** Companion to the playbook (the how), the campaign (the what/when) and `launch-scripts-v1` (the four locked launch videos).

**How to use:** each batch session, pick ~4 across different pillars, confirm the real numbers in the tool, then film. Every idea is a **finding, not a sell** — the pillar's tool sells itself once someone lands on it. Tick items as you ship them; add new ones as tools/data grow.

**The non-negotiables (from the playbook + rev-22 audio rule):** faceless; one idea per video; hook in 2 seconds; brand only at the end; **every number confirmed live in the tool before filming** (this is a "we run the numbers" brand — a wrong figure is the one thing that breaks it); sound-off first (captions carry it) but every published video gets audio (music bed + SFX; VO optional). Max 1 tool-demo in 6 — these are findings, so that cap is rarely in play.

**Data-readiness legend:** ✅ locked (real data already captured) · 🔎 needs a quick tool check before filming · 🏗️ blocked on a build/data task (noted).

**Tool → pillar map:** Cabin Verdicts = Good Cabin Bad Cabin · The Bar Tab = GetMyBarTab · Connectivity = GetMyCruiseConnection · Port Intel = GetMyCruiseWeather (+ Shore Thing when un-hidden) · Casino Points = Sea You Soon · (Leave at Home / gear = All Aboard Store, used as a finding).

## Pillar 1 — Cabin Verdicts (Good Cabin Bad Cabin) · Series: CABIN COURT

The flagship pillar — endless material because every ship has a best, a worst, and a trap.

1. **✅ The worst oceanview on Sun Princess (#186 of 186)** — *this is L1, locked.* Cabin 6202, 55.8/100, under the theatre, bow motion.
2. **🔎 Same price, opposite verdict.** Two cabins one row apart — 6202 (55.8, last) vs 6204 (61.6). "One row forward, £0 difference, and one's the worst on the ship." The contrast/comparison format is the playbook's saves-machine. *Ready to film off the same screenshot set as L1.*
3. **🔎 The best-value balcony on [ship].** Flip the negativity — find the highest-scoring balcony that *isn't* a suite price. Positive verdicts get saved-for-later just as hard as horror stories.
4. **🔎 "Guarantee cabin" roulette.** Pick a low-scoring cabin and frame it as "this is what a guarantee booking can land you." Book-a-guarantee is a common cruiser gamble — high relatability.
5. **🔎 Under the pool deck: the 6am dragging-chairs cabin.** Find a cabin directly below the Lido/pool deck and show the score hit. Universally understood pain point.
6. **🔎 Connecting-door cabins nobody warns you about.** Surface a cabin flagged for a connecting door / adjacent noise and explain the score. "The floor plan detail that costs you sleep."

## Pillar 2 — The Bar Tab (GetMyBarTab) · Series: THE TAB

Money maths, itemised — inherently shareable because everyone's wondered "is the package worth it?"

7. **✅ The £41 question.** *This is L3, locked.* Princess, £579 tab, £41.32/day break-even, bundle named honestly.
8. **🔎 The port-day trap.** Zoom in on just the sea-vs-port split (£399 sea vs £180 port on a Princess week): "You pay full package price on the 3 days you're off the ship barely drinking." One sharp idea, very screenshot-able.
9. **🔎 The coffee-and-water package myth.** Show how much of a "bar tab" is actually non-alcoholic (bottled water £32 + specialty coffee £49 on the Princess run). "Half the reason people buy the drinks package isn't even alcohol."
10. **🔎 Light drinker vs heavy drinker: who should buy the package.** Run the tool twice (Light vs Heavy profile) and show the break-even flip. Honest, useful, drives people to run *their own* numbers.
11. **🔎 The "every adult must buy it" rule.** The playbook's own insight: big lines force every adult in the cabin onto the same package, so the *lightest* drinker's number decides it. Great counterintuitive hook: "The package isn't priced for you — it's priced for whoever drinks least."
12. **🏗️ Royal Caribbean, drinks-only: the clean comparison.** RC's Deluxe package is drinks-only (no WiFi/tips bundle), so it's a tidier "does it pay?" video — *blocked until real RC bar prices are curated into GetMyBarTab* (currently generic fallback). Backlog item; re-cut L3-style once done.

## Pillar 3 — Connectivity (GetMyCruiseConnection) · Series: SIGNAL CHECK

The most *shareable* pillar — pure counterintuitive myth-busting. (Commission not yet wired, but content builds the audience now.)

13. **🔎 Your travel eSIM dies the second you leave port.** The flagship myth-bust: Airalo/Holafly work in port, not at open sea. "The £20 eSIM everyone recommends is useless on a sea day." *Was the original L2 — strong enough to headline a later batch.*
14. **🔎 What actually works at sea vs what only works in port.** A two-column finding: satellite ship WiFi (works, pricey) vs port-only eSIM/roaming (free-ish, dead at sea). Clean, saveable reference.
15. **🔎 Starlink at sea: which lines have it, which don't.** Cruisers massively overestimate onboard WiFi. Findings video listing which fleets have fast satellite WiFi now. *Confirm current line-by-line status before filming — this changes fast.*
16. **🔎 The "free WiFi in port" trick.** Where to actually get online for free on a port day (terminal WiFi, café chains) vs burning ship WiFi credit. Practical, savey.
17. **🔎 Airplane-mode-or-£100-bill.** The roaming horror: one forgotten phone connecting to the ship's maritime cell tower. "The setting that saves you a £100 bill before you've left the cabin."
18. **🔎 How much onboard WiFi really costs per day, per device.** Run the real numbers on a week of ship WiFi for a couple with 2 devices each. Bill-shock reveal.

Weather + port findings. Strong because it defies the brochure with data.

19. **✅ The Paris day nobody packs for (wind).** *This is L4, locked.* Le Havre 31 Oct, 74% wind, 34% warmth.
20. **🔎 The "sunny Med" port that's a coin-flip.** Find a shoulder-season Med port (Santorini/Dubrovnik/Barcelona early-Oct) where rain risk is ~50% against its endless-summer reputation. *Check the tool for the biggest expectation-vs-reality gap first.*

## Cross-pillar & format notes
- **Contrast videos out-save straight verdicts** (idea 2, 10) — the playbook flags saves/shares as the growth currency, so lean into "A vs B, same price" framings across every pillar.
- **Positive surprises balance the negativity** (ideas 3, 21) — a feed of only "here's what's terrible" reads cynical; mix in "here's the hidden gem".
- **Tool-literacy videos** (ideas 24) are the sanctioned soft-demo slot (1 in 6).
- **Cross-pillar links** (idea 29 = Casino × Bar Tab) show the estate hangs together — good for the "one place for all of it" brand.
