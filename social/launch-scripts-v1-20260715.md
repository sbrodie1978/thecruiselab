## L1 — "The worst oceanview on Sun Princess"
**P1 Cabin Verdicts · Series: CABIN COURT · ~25s · Good Cabin Bad Cabin**

- **HOOK (0–3s):** *"This is the worst oceanview cabin on Sun Princess. Out of 186."* — cabin 6202 + "#186 of 186" on screen.
- **BODY (3–17s):** deck-plan zoom to 6202 (Deck 6, forward, port); three real reasons as stat cards:
  - *Directly above you: the Princess Arena theatre.*
  - *Right up at the bow, Deck 6 — Motion scores just 39/100. You'll feel every swell.*
  - *212 sq ft — Space & Comfort 43/100.*
- **VERDICT (17–21s):** score dial hits **55.8 · BAD CABIN** stamp; *"Dead last of every oceanview on the ship."*
- **CTA (21–25s):** *"Score your exact cabin before you book — free. Link in bio."* → flask → thecruiselab.com → Good Cabin Bad Cabin.
- **Audio:** low tense bed; *whoosh* on the deck-plan zoom; subtle **swell/creak** SFX under the motion card; heavy **stamp thunk** on BAD CABIN; resolve sting. VO (opt): *"Same money as any oceanview… the worst one on the ship."*
- **Real figures (Good Cabin Bad Cabin, confirmed):** Sun Princess · cabin 6202 · oceanview · 212 sq ft · Deck 6 · port · forward · **55.8/100 (BAD CABIN)** · Quietness 68 · Motion 39.2 · Convenience 58.7 · Space & Comfort 43.4 · directly above = Princess Arena (theatre) · **#186 of 186 oceanview cabins** · #2138 of 2157 shipwide.
- **Accuracy guardrails:** use the tool's **/100 scale** (55.8), not /10. Frame the theatre as a **location fact**, NOT "noisy" — Quietness actually scored 68, so a noise claim contradicts the tool. The hook rests on the **#186-of-186 ranking**, which is exact and checkable.

## L2 — "5 things they'll confiscate at the gangway"
**Leave at Home · Series: LAB REPORT · ~25s · All Aboard Store (commission tool, framed as a finding)**

- **HOOK (0–3s):** *"Pack these and security takes them at the gangway."* — no sell, pure warning.
- **BODY (3–18s):** countdown of banned-onboard items, one b-roll + one line each — **surge protector · iron/steamer · candles · kettle · drone**. #1 held longest.
- **VERDICT (18–22s):** *"There's a right version of half of these. We list them."*
- **CTA (22–25s):** *"The full 'leave at home' list — free. Link in bio."* → flask → thecruiselab.com → All Aboard Store.
- **Audio:** light bed with a slight "uh-oh" edge; a **buzzer/confiscate** SFX on each item; small resolve on the end card. VO (opt): *"Five things that won't make it past the gangway."*
- **Why it works:** lands people on the Store (where the Amazon links + "right version" products live) via a **rules-finding, not a shopping pitch** — serves the 180-day Amazon sales clock without being a demo (stays within the playbook's "1 in 6" demo cap because it isn't one). List confirmed against the Store's own "Leave at Home" anti-catalogue.

## L3 — "The £41 question"
**P2 The Bar Tab · Series: THE TAB · ~30s · GetMyBarTab (Princess, real curated prices)**

- **HOOK (0–3s):** *"Two people. 7-night Princess cruise. Here's your real bar tab."*
- **BODY (3–19s):** the tool's receipt prints line by line (screen-record the real Princess tab):
  - *Bottled water £32.44 · Specialty coffee £48.66 · Beer/cider/lager £259.52 · Wine £237.90*
  - *Service charge (18%): £88.25*
  - **TAB TOTAL: £579** — lands big.
  - Twist card: *"That's £41 a day, each."*
- **THE HONEST BEAT (19–24s):** *"Yes, Princess Plus throws in WiFi and tips too. So judge it on the drinks alone — and your drinks are £41 a day."*
- **VERDICT (24–28s):** *"For two medium drinkers, the drinks don't get you there. And you'd still pay full whack on 3 port days you're barely drinking."* (£399 sea vs £180 port)
- **CTA (28–30s):** *"Run your real bar tab before you buy — free. Link in bio."* → flask → thecruiselab.com → GetMyBarTab.
- **Audio:** light upbeat bed; **receipt-tick/register** SFX per printed line (made for sound-on); **cha-ching** on £579; small beat-drop on the "£41 a day" twist. VO (opt): *"Two medium drinkers, seven nights… and even stripping out the WiFi and tips, the drinks don't pay."*
- **Real figures (GetMyBarTab, Princess, confirmed):** 7 nights · 3 port days · 2 guests · both medium beer-&-wine, a bit less in port · Bottled water £32.44 · Specialty coffee £48.66 · Beer/cider/lager £259.52 · Wine £237.90 · drinks subtotal £490.27 · 18% service £88.25 · **TAB TOTAL £579** · per person £289 (~£41/day) · **break-even £41.32/day** · sea days £399 vs port days £180.
- **Accuracy guardrails:** the tool is **line-blind on package prices** — do NOT put a specific Princess package £ on screen as if the tool said it; frame as "beat £41/day or it's losing you money" (the tool's own wording). The £41.32 break-even is specific to **two medium drinkers** — anchor the claim to that profile (a heavy-cocktails couple would blow past it and the package *would* pay). Name the **Plus bundle** (WiFi + tips) honestly — this pre-empts the "you ignored the bundle" comment and is a trust signal, not a weakness.
- **Note:** Royal Caribbean was considered (clean drinks-only package = tidier comparison) but GetMyBarTab currently uses **generic "typical cruise bar prices" for RC, not real RC-specific prices** ("prices are coming" per the tool's own UI) — so RC data isn't yet defensible for a "we run the numbers" video. Princess is the only individually-curated line in v1. **Future upgrade:** curate real RC bar prices into a `LINES` override, then re-cut L3 on RC for the cleaner comparison.

## L4 — "The Paris day nobody packs for"
**P4 Port Intel · Series: PORT FILE · ~25s · GetMyCruiseWeather**

- **HOOK (0–3s):** *"Le Havre for a Paris day trip. Late October. You packed for this."* — golden-autumn Paris b-roll.
- **TURN (3–8s):** *"We pulled the last 5 years for that exact date."* — postcard shot starts blowing.
- **BODY (8–19s):** screen-record the real tool output:
  - *Wind exposure: 74%. "Frequently windy."*
  - *4 of the last 5 years: 28–37 km/h gusts.*
  - *Warmth confidence just 34% — highs of 14°C.*
- **VERDICT (19–23s):** *"It's not a rain jacket you forgot. It's a windbreaker."*
- **CTA (23–25s):** *"Check your exact port date — free. Link in bio."* → flask → thecruiselab.com → GetMyCruiseWeather.
- **Audio:** breezy bed → colder/gustier on the turn; rising **wind-gust whoosh** as the postcard flaps (signature beat); *tick/stamp* on each stat card; resolve sting. VO (opt): *"Everyone packs for a Paris autumn. Nobody packs for the wind off the Channel."*
- **Real figures (GetMyCruiseWeather, Le Havre / Paris, 31 Oct, confirmed):** Warmth confidence **34%** ("often cold") · Rain risk 46% ("moderate") · **Wind exposure 74%** ("frequently windy") · 5-year snapshot: 2025 17°/12°/0mm/29kmh · 2024 14°/9°/0mm/14kmh · 2023 14°/10°/10.6mm/31kmh · 2022 18°/11°/7.7mm/28kmh · 2021 14°/11°/12.2mm/37kmh (→ 4 of 5 years 28–37 km/h).
- **Accuracy guardrails:** the tool uses the **last 5 years**, so say "5 years," not 10. Led with **wind (74%, 4 of 5 years)**, NOT rain — rain is a coin-flip (46%, two dry years), so a rain hook would overclaim. Every figure is pulled straight from the tool and defensible.
